## Executive Summary

A brain-inspired learning system that predicts failures before they occur and recursively improves its own learning capabilities. Based on three key research papers (predictive coding, interaction prediction, active hierarchical models), this system shifts from reactive problem-solving to proactive prevention.

**Core Principle:** Don't just learn from mistakes—predict and prevent them. Don't just remember—learn how to learn better.

## Research Foundation

### 1. Predictive Coding Networks (PCNs)
- **Paper:** Predictive Coding Networks and Inference Learning (arxiv.org/abs/2407.04117)
- **Key Insight:** Brain minimizes prediction errors through hierarchical feedback
- **Our Application:** Energy-efficient anomaly detection with few-shot learning

### 2. Learning by Predicting Interaction (MPI)
- **Paper:** Learning Manipulation by Predicting Interaction (arxiv.org/abs/2406.00439)
- **Key Insight:** Predict "how-to-interact" and "where-to-interact"
- **Our Application:** API/codebase interaction prediction, failure cascade mapping

### 3. Active Predictive Coding (APC)
- **Paper:** Active Predictive Coding (arxiv.org/abs/2210.13461)
- **Key Insight:** Hierarchical world models for perception and planning
- **Our Application:** Multi-level abstraction, cross-domain intelligence transfer
